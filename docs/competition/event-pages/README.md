## The reference triangle

- **Structure & restraint:** Stripe Sessions 2026 (viewport-scale sentence hero, dates as drafting metadata, dim-to-lit manifesto, ruled sections).
- **Register & authority:** Black Ambition (black/white/gold, manifesto confidence, "changing the landscape of who is investable" — institutional warmth).
- **Budget-grandeur proof:** WIP Bilbao (plotter marks, giant date numerals, grain — all reproducible in CSS) + SITCON 2026 (nearly our palette, ships reduced-motion).
- **Energy garnish:** AfroTech's speaker-wall color rhythm and slow ticker mantras — borrowed at lower voltage.

## The rules distilled (encoded in `copy/08-event-motion-copy.md` + the `ventriq-design` skill)

1. **Voltage 4–5 of 10** — "a gala invitation with a festival heartbeat." Midnight is the room, gold is the engraving.
2. **One festival gesture per page** (Summit: the gold ticker; FAH: the tally-draw) — never stacked.
3. **Countdown = drafting metadata** in the hero meta row, monospace, tabular — never a billboard.
4. **The corridor-draw is the differentiator** — no benchmark page does scroll-drawn converging line-work. Our signature is an open lane, not an imitation.
5. **The Summit page is a state machine** (pre → announced → live-today → between → replay). An alive page during Aug 10–21 is the real WOW.
6. **Static-first beats the benchmarks** — flagship event sites serve blank pages without JS and rot after their dates. Server-rendered, always-current pages are themselves a premium signal in this lane.
7. Belonging is built with **named people and plural ownership** ("we"), photography with one warm treatment, achievement-first speaker bios — never demographic labels.
8. Keep the hype-lane's conversion mechanics (inline ≤2-field registration feel, trust microcopy, progressive speaker reveal, replay policy in FAQ, post-event waitlist flip) — refuse its skin (borrowed-authority headlines, % claims, ALL-CAPS superlatives, gradient gloss).
