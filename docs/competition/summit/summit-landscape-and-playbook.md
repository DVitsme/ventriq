# Ventriq Summit — Events Landscape & Virtual-Summit Funnel Playbook

Research date: 2026-07-08. Scope: founder-facing event landscape + the virtual-summit-as-funnel playbook for the Aug 10–21, 2026 Ventriq Summit (8 sessions, free, Eventbrite reg, CTA = join Founders After Hours on Skool; goal 1,200 registrations).

## TL;DR

- **Timing verdict: GO.** Aug 10–21 is clear of major national competition for this audience. Only overlap: RenderATL / Atlanta Tech Week (Aug 9–14, in-person, dev-culture crowd) — minimal collision with a free virtual small-biz summit. VERIFIED.
- **August is National Black Business Month** — a free positioning tailwind; the summit can ride existing press/social momentum instead of fighting it. VERIFIED.
- **Name is clear.** No "Ventriq Summit" exists. SEO caution: the query currently surfaces North Face "Summit Ventrix" jackets and Ventra Health — plan schema + Eventbrite/LinkedIn listings to own the SERP. VERIFIED (search-level; no trademark search done).
- Big Black-founder events cluster in **fall** (AfroTech Nov 2–6, DC Startup & Tech Week Oct 19–23, CultureCon Oct 3–4, TEDCO Expo Oct 27, GEW mid-Nov) — position Ventriq as the **"summer on-ramp before conference season."**
- **Industry-standard funnel:** free live ticket → replays expire in 24–72h → $47–$197 All-Access Pass (3–5% conversion) → membership upsell. **Ventriq's variant — replays gated inside a free Skool community — is the higher-throughput lead-gen play** (~30% of registrants join a community vs. 3–5% buying a pass) and matches the exact model of the Robbins/Graziosi "AI Advantage Summit" (free summit → free Skool 103k members → $37/mo club).
- **Speakers are the traffic engine:** 60–80% of summit registrations typically come from speaker promotion, not the host's list or ads. The speaker promo kit (3 swipe emails + graphics + tracking links) matters more than any page element.
- **1,200 registrations is plausible** if ~8 speakers with real email lists each send 2–3 emails (documented first-time summits: 1,600–11,500 regs without paid ads). ESTIMATE.
- **Attendance reality:** ~40–50% of webinar registrants show live (single event); for multi-day summits expect 30–50% to attend *at least one* session and far fewer per session (~5–15%). Design the funnel so no-shows still convert (replay CTA → Skool).
- **No-show mitigation:** calendar file in confirmation (+4–7 pts show-up), 48h/24h/1–3h email cadence (Eventbrite automates the 48h one), day-of "we're live" email; "playbacks only in the community" is itself an attendance/join driver.
- **Biggest anti-pattern to avoid:** vague "for all business owners" positioning. Niche the promise (underrepresented founders, one concrete outcome per day) and say out loud that it's only ~1 hour/day — an anti-fatigue feature.

## Part B — The Virtual-Summit-Funnel Playbook

### B1. Standard mechanics (2025–26 norm)

1. **Landing page with one job:** free ticket signup. HeySummit claims "industry-leading signup rates of 28% and above"; Summit In A Box (SIAB) reports registration-page conversion of 20–83% and coaches to "at least 50%." VERIFIED sources, ESTIMATE applicability.
2. **Thank-you page = the money page:** immediate upsell (All-Access Pass with deadline timer) + refer-a-friend. In a HeySummit case study the free ticket was the *only* option on the page; the AAP appeared post-registration with a Deadline Funnel — 460 of 1,600 registrants (~29%) took a $49 pass (Ultimate CIRS Summit — outlier niche; industry standard is 3–5%). VERIFIED.
3. **Speakers promote via swipe kit** (announcement email / "my session is live today" / "last chance"), usually as affiliates earning 30–50% of AAP sales. Speaker promotion drives 60–80% of registrations. VERIFIED (Matt McWilliams, EHQ, TheSummitHost).
4. **Free replay scarcity:** sessions free live, replays expire after 24–72h (Foundr: VIP-only recordings; Brand Builders Summit: "Free attendees will have 72 hour access to each presentation"; Rebel Boss: 24h). VERIFIED.
5. **Paid AAP $47–$197** (SIAB: min $47, max ~$197; Rebel Boss: $97, $197 w/ bonus stack; 10x value-stacking norm) → then evergreen replays or membership/community upsell. VERIFIED.
6. **Skool-era variant (Hormozi ecosystem):** the **AI Advantage Summit** (Robbins/Graziosi, Apr 23–25, 2026): free 3-day summit → free Skool community (103,200+ members) where resources/replay-adjacent content live → $1 trial → $37/mo "AI Amplifier Club," pitched on Day 3. Free community = funnel for paid tier ("free community serves as a funnel for your premium offering" — Hormozi monetization norm). VERIFIED. **This is Ventriq's model with different logos.**

### B3. Recommended funnel shape for Ventriq (vs. paid-replay standard)

**Recommended:** Eventbrite free ticket → instant confirmation w/ calendar files + **immediate Skool invite** ("claim your seat in Founders After Hours now — that's where playbacks land") → ESP email sequence (Eventbrite alone is not enough; export/sync registrants to the email tool daily) → live Zoom sessions → replay posted in Skool ~24h later, **members-only** → advisor office hours/webinars retain them.

**Trade-off vs. selling an All-Access Pass:** an AAP at $97 × 1,200 regs × 3–5% ≈ **$3.5–5.8k gross** (ESTIMATE) — real money, but it (a) splits the CTA, (b) competes with the Skool join, and (c) monetizes exactly the people Ventriq wants inside the community. The community-gated-replay converts ~30% vs 3–5% — roughly **10x the funnel throughput** for a lead-gen goal. Skip the paid pass for v1. Optional later hybrid: a $47–$97 "Founder Toolkit" (downloadable recordings + workbooks + priority Q&A) that does NOT gate the community, and/or a paid Skool tier once the free tier is warm (Hormozi free→paid ladder).

**Keep the scarcity mechanics anyway:** "replays available only inside Founders After Hours, 24h after each session" preserves urgency without a paywall; live-only Q&A stays exclusive to drive attendance.

### B4. Summit page blueprint for Derrick (ordered sections + rationale)

Synthesized from Foundr (Jul 2026), Brand Builders Summit (Sep 2026), EHQ's 9-element checklist, HeySummit patterns:

1. **Hero** — outcome headline + "Free · Virtual · Aug 10–21" + primary CTA + countdown. One CTA only (free ticket). Foundr repeats its CTA 10+ times down-page; do the same.
2. **Credibility strip** — partner/press/affiliation logos ("As seen in…" or "Backed by/Partners"). Instant-trust per EHQ; for a first-year event use speaker-company logos.
3. **Stats bar** — "8 Speakers · 8 Sessions · 2 Weeks · 1 Hour a Day · 100% Free" (pattern: Foundr's "14+ Founders / 15+ Masterclass Hours / 7+ Key Growth Areas"). The "1 hour a day" line is the anti-fatigue differentiator — say it.
4. **What you'll learn** — 3–4 topic pillars mapping to the session themes (Brand Builders' three-pillar section).
5. **Speaker wall** — grid: photo, name, company, session title + day. Photos of real humans are the #1 scanned element.
6. **Agenda by day** — two week-blocks (Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri), each day = date, speaker, topic, one-line takeaway, add-to-calendar link.
7. **How it works** — 3 steps: ① Register free (Eventbrite) ② Show up live for the talk + Q&A ③ Playbacks + resources live inside Founders After Hours. This is where the community CTA is seeded *before* the summit.
8. **Who it's for** — name the audience explicitly (underrepresented founders, small-business owners, nonprofit builders; "DMV-rooted, open everywhere"). Foundr's inclusive-eligibility pattern.
9. **Host story** — Justin Shaw / why Ventriq exists (mission credibility; nonprofits must over-index here).
10. **Social proof** — pre-event: speaker quotes about why they said yes; post-event: attendee quotes + a "% would recommend" stat (Brand Builders publishes "100% of attendees would recommend," 300+ survey responses).
11. **FAQ** — is it really free / do I get replays (→ community answer) / how much time / who it's for / is it live / how do I ask questions / what's Founders After Hours. Mirror Foundr's 6-Q and Brand Builders' 13-Q sets.
12. **Final CTA** — repeat hero promise + countdown.
Thank-you page: calendar buttons (Google/Outlook/iCal separately), Skool join button as THE next step, refer-a-friend line.

### B6. Speaker-recruitment leverage (what speakers get / are asked)

- **The ask (norm):** one 45–60 min session + send 2–3 pre-written emails to their list + 2 social posts. Provide a promo kit: swipe copy, per-speaker graphics, personalized links, and a send-date calendar. VERIFIED norms (EHQ/SIAB).
- **What they get (norm):** exposure to a new audience; a lead-gen slot (their freebie/link featured on the session page and in the session email); the recording as a content asset; 30–50% affiliate commission where an AAP exists. With no AAP, Ventriq substitutes: featured AMA/office-hour in Founders After Hours, "speaker spotlight" email to all registrants, and visible positioning alongside the other 7 speakers.
- **Recruiting filter:** because speakers drive 60–80% of registrations, pick at least 4–5 of the 8 for their *engaged email lists/communities*, not title prestige. Warm intros over cold outreach (EHQ).

## Risks & Anti-Patterns

- **Vague positioning kills summits:** "a summit for all business owners who want to grow their own business… won't hit them hard enough to make them want to participate" (SIAB). Niche the promise per day.
- **Event ≠ funnel:** "That's not a summit funnel — that's an expensive conference that might get you a list of people who like free content and don't buy anything" (Scale For Impact). Every session ends in the same single CTA (join Founders After Hours), rehearsed with speakers.
- **Low-value perception, not fatigue:** "People aren't tired of signing up for free events — they're tired of signing up for low-value ones" (Summit Success Squad, 2026). Recycled generic topics are the tell; require each speaker to teach one specific, doable play.
- **Content overload:** cramming sessions forces choices and burns attendees; Ventriq's 1-session/day format is the cure — market it as such.
- **Two-week momentum decay (ESTIMATE, my analysis):** most summits run 3–5 days precisely to hold attention. Mitigate: weekend "week 1 highlights + what's coming" email, week-2-only bonus (live group Q&A/office hour), daily activity inside Skool between sessions.
- **No-show mitigation stack:** calendar files in every email (Google/Outlook/iCal separate links); email cadence 1 week / 48h (Eventbrite auto) / 24h / 3h / "we're live now"; SMS optional and strictly logistical ("The moment a text starts teaching, opt-outs spike"); micro-commitments (ask registrants to post an intro in Skool pre-event) raise show-up intent. VERIFIED sources.
- **Eventbrite dependency:** registrant emails sit in Eventbrite — sync daily to the ESP and put the Skool invite in the Eventbrite confirmation itself, or half the funnel never sees it.
- **August vacations (ESTIMATE):** schedule for lunch (12pm ET) or evening; lean on national reach + replays.
