# National/Online Mastermind & Peer-Group Market — Competitive Research
**For:** Ventriq Mastermind (90-day, ≤10-founder cohort, weekly Zoom, dashboard, 1:1 advisor) · **Researched:** 2026-07-08 · **Lane:** national/online (DMV-regional covered separately)
**Labels:** VERIFIED = seen on primary source or official page · ESTIMATE = third-party/press-derived · UNKNOWN = not findable

---

## TL;DR

1. The market prices **intimacy + facilitation**, not content. Content is ~$47–$99/mo (Mastermind.com, Skool); a facilitated small peer group is $3.5k–$22k/yr (TAB, EO, Hampton, Vistage).
2. **Every premium player hides its price** behind an application (Hampton, Vistage, C12, Baby Bathwater, SaaS Academy). Publishing eligibility while hiding price IS the premium signal. Ventriq's "investment invoice" matches the category playbook exactly.
3. **Weekly cadence is Ventriq's structural edge**: EO Forum, Hampton, Vistage, TAB, C12 all meet ~monthly. Nobody in the set runs a weekly small-group container.
4. **Nobody measures anything.** Vistage cites one 2020 macro stat; GS 10KSB publishes longitudinal alumni stats; no mastermind tallies "systems installed" or "hours reclaimed." Ventriq's dashboard is the only per-member proof engine in the category.
5. Ventriq's format most resembles **"EO Forum intimacy × accelerator sprint × Vistage accountability"** — that combination at sub-$1M revenue doesn't exist. EO Accelerator ($1,750/yr, $250k–$999k rev) is the closest gated product and it's slow-cadence (monthly-ish groups, quarterly learning days).
6. Supportable corridor for a 90-day gated cohort with weekly facilitation + 1:1 + dashboard: **~$2,500–$7,500 per cohort** (≈$800–$2,500/mo effective). Below ~$1.5k it reads like a Skool group; above ~$8k it collides with Hampton/Vistage annual dues and their $1M–$3M-revenue buyers.
7. **Free anchors are curriculum, not community**: GS 10KSB (free, 12–14 wk, application-gated — Justin's alumni comp set) and SCORE (free mentoring) prove education is free; the paid thing must be the room, the cadence, and the accountability.
8. Underrepresented-founder programs are almost all **free, corporate-sponsored, VC-track** (Google, AWS, Barclays) — no one owns "premium gated peer group for underrepresented main-street founders."
9. Faith angle: only C12 owns faith + business nationally (monthly, Vistage-priced, explicitly Christian). A faith-undertone (not faith-gated) mastermind is open space.
10. Steal the Hampton page: problem-headline ("Stop making $50M decisions alone"), "personal board of ~10 vetted founders," numbered application steps incl. member veto, show-up-conditional 45-day guarantee, named-founder proof.

## Per-competitor profiles

### Hampton (joinhampton.com) — the model to study
- **Format:** "Your personal board of ~10 vetted founders, meeting monthly in your city" (verbatim). Monthly ~3-hr professionally facilitated core group (no Aug/Dec) + 1,000+-member Slack + chapter dinners + optional at-cost retreats. VERIFIED (site, 2026-07-08).
- **Price:** hidden on site ("We've structured our dues to make it a no-brainer"). $8,500/yr all-inclusive widely reported 2023–25 (Unita, Long Angle, press); one 2024 article claimed $3k–$5k tiers. Treat as **ESTIMATE: ~$8.5k/yr**.
- **Eligibility (verbatim):** "$3M+ annual revenue," "$3M+ in funding raised," or "previously sold a startup for $10M+." Raised from $1M in 2024 (Parr on X).
- **Positioning (verbatim):** "If you're a founder… Stop making $50M decisions alone." / "Lonely decisions are expensive. Stop paying that tax." / "you can't get this stuff on Google."
- **Entry:** apply → screen → vetting interview → community veto period → founder approval → 10-day acceptance window; core-group placement within ~90 days. Acceptance rate ~5–10% (3rd-party ESTIMATE).
- **Guarantee:** "45-day money-back guarantee" IF "you show up for your initial Commitment Call. No hoops, no headaches."
- **Proof:** named members with company/exit sizes (Morning Brew, Figure AI; $75M–$110M exits); case studies with time-boxed outcomes ("hired a VP in 14 days").

### Entrepreneurs' Organization + EO Accelerator (eonetwork.org)
- **EO full:** 19k+ members; Forum = "7 to 10 members meeting monthly," trained peer moderator, **Gestalt protocol — experience-sharing only, advice banned**. Dues US$2,470/yr global + US$3,500 one-time initiation + chapter dues; $1M+ revenue gate. VERIFIED (helloeo.org, member.eonetwork.org, 2026-07-08).
- **EO Accelerator:** "$1,750 USD, covering July 1 to June 30" (verbatim, FAQ); for founders "$250,000–$999,999 USD" aiming "to grow their business past the $1M USD mark"; four full-day learning events/yr + "8–12 accountability group meetings (usually 2–4 hours per month)"; designed as 2-year program. **Outcome stat (verbatim style): 10,000+ EOA grads, ~2,000 joined EO; "average time to reach $1M is 24 months."** VERIFIED (eonetwork.org/accelerator/faqs, 2026-07-08).
- **Takeaway for Ventriq:** EOA is the closest gated national product for sub-$1M founders — 4x cheaper than Ventriq will likely be, but far lower touch (no weekly cadence, no dashboard, no 1:1 advisor).

### Vistage (vistage.com)
- **Format:** groups of "12–16 business leaders from non-competing companies," monthly day-long boards, Chair = "part facilitator, part coach," plus 1:1 coaching and speaker workshops. VERIFIED (site, 2026-07-08).
- **Price:** not on site. $10,500–$16,500/yr + $2,250 initiation (FounderGroups, undated); ~$2,350/mo and $13k–$22k/yr ranges elsewhere. **ESTIMATE: $13k–$22k/yr all-in.**
- **Proof (verbatim):** members "grew their annual revenue on average by 4.6% in 2020, while non-member businesses saw revenue decrease by 4.7%"; "65+ years," "45,000 members."
- **Entry:** qualification form → sales process ("Complete the form below to find out if you qualify").

### YPO (ypo.org) — context only
$8,910/yr total dues + $12,650 total initiation (FY ending 6/30/2027, chapter-dependent); gate: became CEO before 45 of a $15M+-revenue company (or $25M enterprise value, 50 FTE). VERIFIED via ypo.org requirements. Irrelevant as a direct comp; useful as ceiling reference.

### Founders Network (foundersnetwork.com)
Tiered open-ish community for tech founders: Launch $75/mo, Scale $134/mo, Lead $249/mo (billed annually; VERIFIED site 2026-07-08); ~$699/yr entry and $10k lifetime reported by directories. 600+ vetted tech founders, monthly peer roundtables only at Lead tier. Low-friction "request invite." No guarantees. Volume-style proof ("helped 2,000+ startups").

### Dynamite Circle (dynamitecircle.com)
- $697/yr (VERIFIED, 2026-07-08). "Connect with 1000+ global founders running profitable, location-independent businesses." 1,400+ members, 50+ countries.
- Masterminds of 4–8 founders "matched by revenue stage." Gate: $100k+ revenue; **exclusion list verbatim: no business coaches, lifestyle coaches, brokers, recruiters, consultants, freelancers.** Applications "personally reviewed within 48 business hours."
- **DC BLACK:** $5,500/yr (ESTIMATE via site/3rd-party, 2025–26) for 7–8-figure founders (avg $4M+ rev): monthly facilitated 5–7-person mastermind, coaching, exclusive events. Clean example of a two-tier ladder: cheap community → expensive facilitated mastermind.

### Baby Bathwater Institute (babybathwater.com)
Invitation-only, event-first: attend a 4–5-day event, ~20% get invited to membership. "Equal parts retreat, think tank, and party"; "underground network of amazing entrepreneurs who have your back"; anti-pitch culture ("you will never be pitched"). Gate: $1M+ rev OR $500k+ profit OR $3M+ raised OR exit. Membership price UNKNOWN (member quote: "tens of thousands over 6.5 years"); flagship events cost thousands (members save "$6,000+ per event" at 50% off). Proof = video testimonials about friendship, not revenue.

### Skool ecosystem + Hormozi's Skool Games (skool.com)
- Platform: $9/mo Hobby, $99/mo Pro per community (VERIFIED, 2026).
- **Member-pricing norms for paid Skool groups (2026 benchmark data): $9–$19/mo = ~41% of paid Skools; $29–$49 = ~36%; $79–$199 = ~17%; $200+/mo = ~6%. Cohort programs sold into communities run $97–$297.** Typical founder-group profile: 180–320 members at $29–$59/mo.
- Skool Games = quarterly 30-day community-growth competition with Hormozi (free to enter with a paid Skool). Positioning is volume/leaderboard/prize-driven — the cultural opposite of gated masterminds.
- **Takeaway:** open-enrollment online "founder community" price ceiling is ~$200/mo; anything above requires vetting + facilitation to justify.

### Mastermind.com (Graziosi/Robbins) + guru tier
- Mastermind.com pricing page (VERIFIED 2026-07-08): Success $47/mo ($423/yr), Business $97/mo ($997/yr), $1 trial. It's now a course/AI/funnel SaaS; "mastermind" is brand language. Upsell path to $1k–$10k+ intensives (3rd-party). Trustpilot/ConsumerAffairs complaints about trial-to-subscription billing — trust liability in this corridor.
- Guru masterminds (ESTIMATE, 2025 reports): Genius Network $25k–$100k/yr; War Room $20k–$50k/yr (folding after Oct 2025); Strategic Coach $12k–$60k/yr; Tiger 21 $30k–$35k/yr; SaaS Academy ~$30k/yr behind a book-a-call funnel. What buys: proximity to a celebrity operator + deal flow + luxury settings.

### Free anchors
- **GS 10KSB (10ksbapply.com, goldmansachs.com):** $0 — "100% funded by the Goldman Sachs Foundation." 12–14 weeks, ~one full day/week, MBA-style curriculum + tailored growth plan. Gate: 2+ years operating, revenue ≥$75k–$150k, 2–4+ employees (varies by delivery site). Competitive application. **Outcome stats: 66–67% of grads increase revenue within 6 months (76% by 18 mo); 44–48% add jobs within 6 months; grads "create jobs 4x faster" than peer businesses; 17,000+ alumni.** This is the stat-publishing model to copy — and Justin's cohort peers are exactly Ventriq's warm market.
- **SCORE (score.org):** free, unlimited, confidential mentoring "for the life of your business," all 50 states, remote or in-person. No cohort, no peers, no cadence — mentor-on-demand.
- **Underrepresented-founder programs:** Google for Startups Black Founders (free + credits), AWS Impact Accelerator (free + up to $225k cash/credits, 8 wks), Barclays Black Founder Accelerator (free, 12 wks), BK-XL ($125k for 7% equity). All free-or-funded, all startup/VC-track — none is a paid peer mastermind for revenue-generating service/main-street businesses.

### The Alternative Board (TAB) & C12 — SMB peer-board franchises
- **TAB:** 8–10 non-competing SMB owners, monthly half-day boards + coaching; $3,500–$5,000/yr (ESTIMATE, 2025 franchise disclosures). The mainstream price floor for a facilitated monthly SMB peer board.
- **C12:** Christian CEO forums, monthly full day + 1:1 coaching + annual 360; fees revenue-based and undisclosed, self-benchmarked "cost competitive with best-in-class executive roundtable organizations" (i.e., Vistage tier). ESTIMATE $10k+/yr for CEO level.

### Cohort-program price anchors (90-day-adjacent)
altMBA: $4,950 for a 31-day intensive workshop (VERIFIED, 2025–26 reviews of altmba.com). Reforge: $1,995/yr membership (VERIFIED, Dec 2024 pricing change). On Deck Founders: pay-what-you-can ~$1,000 suggested, formerly ~$2,990 (ESTIMATE, 2025–26). Skool cohorts: $97–$297. 2X (Austin Netzley): 90-day 1:1 accelerator, price undisclosed — proof style worth noting: "100 hrs/wk → 30 hrs/wk while $67k/mo → $256k/mo."

## Price-corridor map (what each tier buys)

| Corridor | Programs | What the money buys | What's missing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **FREE** | GS 10KSB, SCORE, corporate DEI accelerators | Curriculum, mentors, credential, alumni network | Standing small peer group, weekly accountability, post-program cadence |
| **$100–$700/yr** (≈$9–$59/mo) | Skool groups, DC $697, FN Launch | Big-room community, content, events access | Vetting, facilitation, intimacy — churn model |
| **$1k–$3k/yr** | EOA $1,750, Reforge $1,995, FN Lead ~$3k, On Deck ~$1k | Structured curriculum + light accountability groups OR quality network | High touch; speed (EOA counts progress in years) |
| **$3.5k–$6k/yr / one-time** | TAB $3.5–5k, EO ~$4–6k all-in yr 1 amortized, DC BLACK $5.5k, altMBA $4,950 | **Facilitated monthly small group with a revenue gate**; altMBA = intensive sprint | Weekly cadence, measured outcomes, 1:1 advisory (mostly) |
| **$8k–$22k/yr** | Hampton ~$8.5k, YPO ~$8.9k+init, Vistage $13–22k, C12 (est) | Professional facilitation + curation + 1:1 coaching + status + concierge | Defined outcomes; sprint energy; anyone under $1M–$3M revenue |
| **$25k–$100k/yr** | Genius Network, Strategic Coach, Tiger 21, SaaS Academy | Celebrity proximity, deal flow, luxury retreats | Accessibility; implementation structure |

**Where Ventriq fits.** Format (≤10 people, application-gated, professionally guided, 1:1 advisor, weekly cadence, measurable deliverables) delivers **more touch per month than anything below the Hampton/Vistage tier**, aimed at founders those tiers exclude ($100k–$1M-ish revenue, GS-10KSB-profile owners). That supports a **$2,500–$7,500 per 90-day cohort** corridor: above altMBA-adjacent intensity pricing is defensible via weekly facilitation + dashboard + 1:1; $8k+ collides with Hampton/Vistage annual money and overshoots the audience's wallet. A continuity/alumni tier afterward ($50–$150/mo, Skool-corridor) converts sprint energy into recurring revenue — the DC → DC BLACK ladder in reverse. If cohort 1 outcomes are strong, price can walk up the way Hampton walked its revenue gate up ($1M → $3M).

## Positioning whitespace (what nobody in this set owns)

1. **Measured implementation.** No mastermind counts installed systems or reclaimed hours per member. Vistage's macro stat and GS 10KSB's alumni percentages are the only numbers in the category. Ventriq's dashboard → "documented systems installed / hours reclaimed / revenue delta, tallied per founder" is a category-first proof mechanic. Own the phrase-space around "we count what we install."
2. **Anti-burnout as the headline for a peer group.** Dan Martell/2X own "buy back your time" in $30k coaching; DC owns lifestyle-from-anywhere. No gated small-group mastermind leads with "founders who don't have to choose" (growth vs. health/family/faith). Open.
3. **Underrepresented founders, premium-but-accessible.** Corporate programs = free + VC-track; EO/Hampton/Vistage gate at $1M–$3M. A vetted, paid, high-touch group for underrepresented revenue-stage owners has no national owner.
4. **Nonprofit parentage.** Guru corridor carries active trust damage (billing complaints, funnels). "Run by a nonprofit — the invoice funds the mission, nobody profits off your ambition" is a differentiator no competitor can copy, and it licenses the premium "investment" framing without guru stink.
5. **Faith undertone.** C12 = explicitly Christian, monthly, Vistage-priced, older-skewing. Values-forward-but-not-gated is unclaimed between secular EO/Hampton and all-in C12.
6. **90-day sprint with a finish line.** Everything premium is annual continuity; everything sprint-shaped is either free (10KSB) or content (Skool cohorts, altMBA solo-work). A facilitated peer sprint that *graduates* people — mirroring the 10KSB cohort→alumni arc Justin lived — is structurally distinct.

## Steal-worthy patterns for the Ventriq page

**What application-gated pages do that open-enrollment pages don't** (Hampton, DC, BB, EO, Vistage vs. Mastermind.com, Skool):
- Publish **eligibility** loudly; hide **price** (revealed post-acceptance; softened as "dues"/"investment").
- Make the **vetting process itself a feature**: numbered steps, human review ("personally reviewed within 48 business hours" — DC), community veto (Hampton), invite rates (~20% — BB).
- **Exclusion copy** ("not for coaches, brokers, consultants" — DC; "low ego, givers only" — BB) — who it's NOT for does the positioning work.
- **Member-quality proof** (named founders + revenue/exit figures) instead of member-count proof; case studies with time-boxed outcomes.
- **Conditional guarantee**: Hampton refunds 45 days *if you showed up*. Perfect fit for implementation-over-education positioning.
- Open-enrollment tells: price grids, $1 trials, bonuses, urgency timers, volume testimonials — avoid all of it.

**Specific elements to adapt:**
1. **Headline formula** (Hampton): name the expensive lonely moment — "Stop making $50M decisions alone" → Ventriq-scale equivalent; back it with "Lonely decisions are expensive. Stop paying that tax."
2. **"Your personal board of ~10 vetted founders"** — board metaphor for the ≤10 cohort; "professionally facilitated" language.
3. **Outcome stats in GS 10KSB format**: "X% of cohort-1 founders increased revenue within 6 months; avg Y hrs/week reclaimed; Z systems documented" — longitudinal, percentage-based, dashboard-sourced. This is the single highest-leverage asset the site can carry.
4. **Time-to-milestone stat** (EOA: "average time to $1M is 24 months") — Ventriq: "average time to first documented system: N weeks."
5. **Application-steps section** with acceptance-letter ritual + Hampton-style 10-day acceptance window (built-in urgency without discount games).
6. **Eligibility box** with hard thresholds + exclusions; revenue-stage matching promise (DC: "matched by revenue stage").
7. **Protocol as brand** (EO's named "Gestalt protocol") — name Ventriq's session structure (e.g., the pillars as a named weekly protocol) so the method feels proprietary.
8. **FAQ pattern** (Hampton/EOA): cost framed as investment + guarantee terms; time commitment per week; "what happens after 90 days"; who it's for/not for; how selection works; confidentiality.
9. **Anti-pitch culture line** (BB: "you will never be pitched") — signals safety in a market wary of guru funnels.
10. **At-cost transparency** (Hampton: "we don't profit on events") — nonprofit version: publish where the invoice goes.
