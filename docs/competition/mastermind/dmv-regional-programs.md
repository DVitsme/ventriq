# DMV / Baltimore Regional Founder-Support Landscape
**For:** Ventriq Mastermind positioning (cohort 2, Sept 2026) · **Researched:** 2026-07-08 · **Lane:** regional programs a Baltimore/DC founder could join instead of (or alongside) a paid mastermind. National/online masterminds covered separately.
**Confidence tags:** VERIFIED (source dated 2025–26) / ESTIMATE (older source or inferred) / UNKNOWN.

---

## TL;DR

1. **The region is drowning in free, heavily subsidized programs** (Goldman Sachs, Abell, JPMorganChase, M&T, TEDCO, congressional earmarks) — but almost all are one-shot cohorts, curriculum-first, 6–40 people, early-stage. Nobody sells small-N ongoing peer *accountability*.
2. **The only true paid regional analogs are EO Baltimore (~$3.5K init + dues; EO Accelerator $2,550/yr), Vistage ($12K+/yr, $5M+ CEOs), and C12 Maryland (Christian, $5M+ revenue, Frederick/Columbia/Rockville)** — all with revenue bars that exclude Ventriq's likely member ($150K–$1M growth-stage underrepresented founder). That gap is the whitespace.
3. **Techstars Equitech is dead (ended May 2024)**; successor Techstars AI Health Baltimore (JHU/CareFirst) is vertical-specific. UpSurge has quietly dropped "equitech" from its homepage — it's now an ecosystem convener/navigator, not a program operator. (VERIFIED via site fetch 2026-07-08.)
4. **GS 10KSB Baltimore is the 800-lb free gorilla**: $0 cost, Babson curriculum, 14 weeks, 3 cohorts/yr at JHU — and Justin is an alum. Treat it as a feeder ("what's next after 10KSB?"), not a rival.
5. **1863 Ventures closed as a nonprofit (Dec 2024)** → New Majority Ventures, now distributing virtual programming through other orgs. The DMV's flagship "New Majority" in-person program layer has thinned — another gap.
6. **Underrepresented-founder wording has shifted post-2024/25 legal climate**: public/bank-funded programs use economic proxies ("economic disadvantage," "under-resourced," "historically underserved"); private capital keeps "Black and Latinx" or uses "underestimated"/"New Majority."
7. **Most orgs here are partnership candidates, not competitors** — CDFIs (Wacif, LEDC), TEDCO (capital pillar), CLLCTIVLY, university centers, SCORE/SBDC/WBCs. The real competitor set for a paid seat is EO/Vistage/C12 + national online masterminds.
8. **Overlap risk is real but manageable**: MIC's BRITE "empowers minority and underrepresented entrepreneurs" with free mentoring — same audience, same county employer. Needs a differentiation + disclosure story before launch (see §7).
9. **No regional program offers a performance dashboard or metric-tallied implementation accountability.** Zero. That's Ventriq's ownable mechanic.
10. **Pricing anchor:** the market-tested regional price for structured peer growth support is EO Accelerator's $2,550/yr; C12/Vistage prove $12K+/yr is payable at higher revenue. Price the "investment invoice" with those anchors in view.

### Baltimore core
- **GS 10,000 Small Businesses – Baltimore** — Funded by Goldman Sachs + Bloomberg Philanthropies; delivered by Morgan State, JHU, CCBC. One full day/week × 14 weeks at JHU Mt. Washington; cohorts Jan/May/Sept; apply ~3 months ahead, interview-gated. Eligibility (national baseline): 2+ years operating, ~$100K+ revenue, 2+ full-time employees (sources vary $75K–$150K — ESTIMATE). "100% funded... $0 cost to participants." Peer learning + growth-plan capstone, but no post-program accountability structure beyond an alumni network. Justin is an alum — the alumni base is a recruiting pool, not a rival.
- **UpSurge Baltimore** — Homepage (fetched 2026-07-08) positions "a thriving ecosystem for innovation and startup advancement in the Baltimore Region." Runs UpSurge Discover (resource navigation — Justin's own navigator role), Entrepreneur Support Network convenings, Inflection Point Healthcare Summit (June 4, 2026), MD Student Venture Showcase. "Equitech" branding no longer foregrounded. Ecosystem stats it publishes: 536 active companies; $559.8M raised across 77 deals in 2025.
- **Techstars Equitech → Techstars AI Health Baltimore** — Equitech ran 3 cohorts, concluded May 2024. AI Health (JHU + CareFirst anchors; MD Adam Phillips, ex-Equitech) is a 13-week investment accelerator, first cohort 2025, renewed for Baltimore "with more cash for startups" (Technical.ly). Vertical (health/AI) — not a generalist competitor.

Free 6-month accelerator (Miller Center/Santa Clara partnership, two hand-matched mentors); requires 1+ year operating, KPIs, 6-month runway, and a pre-existing IW relationship — slow on-ramp. ## The Free-Alternative Problem (what free programs DON'T give)

A paid Baltimore mastermind competes with a wall of $0 programs. But every free program in this map shares the same five holes — this is the sales script:
1. **Intimacy & curation.** Free cohorts run 6–40+ members chosen for eligibility/optics, not chemistry. Only EO Forums (6–8) and C12 sell curated small rooms — at $1M/$5M revenue bars. A vetted ≤10 room for $150K–$1M founders exists nowhere.
2. **Implementation accountability.** Every free program is curriculum- or capital-first (Babson modules, Miller Center modules, pitch prep). None tracks whether you *did the thing* week over week. **No regional program has anything like a performance dashboard or peer metric tally** (VERIFIED absence across all sites reviewed). Downtown BOOST's "half the storefronts never opened" press is the cautionary tale free programs can't answer.
3. **Continuity.** Free programs are one-shot (5 days to 6 months) with thin alumni follow-through; GS10KSB alumni in particular graduate with a growth plan and no ongoing peer structure — prime Ventriq recruits.
4. **Speed & access.** Free programs are competitive, calendar-locked (apply ~3 months out; 1–3 cohorts/yr), and often pre-gated (IW requires an existing relationship; RUBRIC/Black Ambition closed). A paid program can say yes in a week.
5. **Stage fit.** The free stack clusters at idea/early stage (bootcamps, pitch, storefront grants) or at investable tech (accelerators). The post-revenue owner scaling a services, nonprofit, or non-VC business falls between free early-stage programs and $12K CEO tables.
Counter-risk to respect: in a subsidy-rich region, "why pay?" is the first objection — answer with accountability + intimacy + speed, and price against EO Accelerator ($2,550/yr) rather than against free.

## Underrepresented-Founder Wording Patterns (verbatim; note the legal-climate drift)

- **Economic proxies (public/bank money):** TEDCO — "economically underserved founders and communities," "demonstrate economic disadvantage"; HoCo Higher — "historically under-served entrepreneurs"; DC Chamber — "under-resourced"; Wacif — "low- and moderate-income entrepreneurs... underserved communities."
- **"Underestimated" family (private/philanthropic):** 1863/New Majority — "historically underestimated entrepreneurs," "New Majority founders"; Novella/CVL — "diverse and under-estimated entrepreneurs"; Techstars Equitech — "underestimated founders."
- **Explicit race (private capital & Black-led orgs):** Latimer House — "Black and Latinx founders," "culturally competent"; BGV — "Black/Brown women founders," "under-resourced women founders"; CLLCTIVLY — "Baltimore's Black social economy"; Downtown BOOST — literally "Black-Owned and Operated Storefront Tenancy"; Morgan State — "disparities in business ownership rates... among people of color."
- **Softened umbrella (2025–26 drift):** MIC BRITE — "minority and underrepresented entrepreneurs"; ETC — "underrepresented founders"; UBalt — "inclusive entrepreneurship... especially among underrepresented groups"; UpSurge — dropped "equitech... where all belong" from primary messaging in favor of "innovation and startup advancement" (site fetch 2026-07-08); Techstars retired the Equitech brand entirely.
- **Implication for Ventriq copy:** the durable, low-risk pattern is *"all founders, with focus on"* + economic/geographic proxies + explicit values language. "Underestimated" and "New Majority" are the strongest borrowed terms with regional resonance (Bradley/Cooper lineage).

## Overlap-Risk Note (for Derrick to raise gently with Justin)

- **BRITE (MIC/Howard County EDA)** already markets free mentoring/strategic-planning support to "minority and underrepresented entrepreneurs" — the same segment Ventriq charges. If Ventriq recruits Howard County founders or MIC/BRITE participants, it can read as the program director selling privately to his employer's pipeline.
- **UpSurge Discover navigator role**: he is positioned as a neutral resource-router; routing founders to his own paid mastermind is a perceived conflict even if disclosed.
- **Mitigations to propose:** (1) written employer awareness/blessing before cohort-2 marketing; (2) geographic/stage framing — Ventriq = Baltimore-city-centered, growth-stage, peer accountability (a format MIC doesn't offer) vs. BRITE = Howard County early-stage training; (3) a stated recusal rule (no direct solicitation inside MIC/UpSurge programs; disclose affiliation in bios); (4) lean nonprofit: position the Mastermind as the "council" pillar complementing — and referring into — MIC, TEDCO, GS10KSB rather than substituting for them. Website copy should list these ecosystems as allies, never comparables.

## Whitespace a Paid, Intimate, Implementation-First, Faith-Undertone Mastermind Can Own

1. **The $150K–$1M revenue gap:** below EO's $1M bar and Vistage/C12's $5M bar, above the idea-stage free stack. No regional room exists for this founder — least of all for underrepresented ones (VERIFIED absence in this scan).
2. **Accountability as product:** weekly implementation cadence + performance dashboard + peer tally — literally no regional program measures execution. Position against "certificate programs": *you leave free programs with a plan; you leave Ventriq with results.*
3. **Faith undertone, Baltimore, accessible:** C12 proves the faith-mastermind model pays in Maryland but sits in Frederick/Columbia/Rockville at $5M+ with a doctrinal gate. A faith-*undertone* (not gated), Baltimore-rooted, underrepresented-founder-centered room is unclaimed.
4. **Cross-sector mix (small biz + tech + nonprofit)** mirrors how Baltimore actually networks; every existing program is siloed by vertical or business type. Pair with CLLCTIVLY/nonprofit world for the nonprofit seat.
5. **The "after-program" position:** market to graduates of GS10KSB, BRITE, IW, AccelerateBaltimore as the ongoing council their programs don't provide — turns every free program from competitor into feeder.
6. **Post-2024 language vacuum:** with Equitech retired and 1863 restructured, nobody in Baltimore currently owns the "underestimated founders" banner at the community level. First-mover naming opportunity for the Summit + Mastermind brand.
