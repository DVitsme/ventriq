# Ventriq — Competitive Intelligence

Six-agent research dive, **July 8, 2026**, for the Ventriq website build. Every claim in the reports is URL-cited, date-stamped, and tagged VERIFIED / ESTIMATE / UNKNOWN.

## The verdict in one paragraph

Ventriq launches into a market that is **crowded with free curriculum and vacant exactly where Justin is building**. Nationally, no gated mastermind runs a weekly small-group container and nobody measures per-member outcomes. Regionally, every paid peer room starts at $1M+ revenue and every free program is one-shot curriculum — the $150K–$1M underrepresented growth-stage founder has nowhere to sit. No founder gathering anywhere runs Founders After Hours' "do the work, tally it" format. And the closest organizational analog (1863 Ventures) shut down in Dec 2024 because philanthropy dependence killed it — which makes Justin's 10%-tithe self-funding model the sharpest donor story in the category. Timing is favorable on every axis checked: the Aug 10–21 summit window is clear, August is National Black Business Month, the "Ventriq Summit" name is unclaimed, and Baltimore's "underestimated founders" banner is currently unowned.

## The whitespace stack (what Ventriq can own — each confirmed independently)

1. **The format:** an in-person founder night where you *do the work and tally the results* — no one runs it, anywhere. (communities)
2. **The cadence:** weekly facilitated small-group at growth stage — every national gated program is ~monthly. (national masterminds)
3. **The segment:** $150K–$1M revenue underrepresented founders — excluded by EO/Vistage/C12 revenue bars, underserved by free one-shot programs. (DMV)
4. **The proof:** per-member outcome measurement ("systems installed, hours reclaimed") — category-first; only GS 10KSB publishes anything comparable. (national masterminds)
5. **The roof:** SMBs + tech startups + nonprofits served together — Fast Forward does nonprofits only, YC does startups, CDFIs do main street only. (nonprofit orgs)
6. **The pillar:** persistent advisory ("Council") — competitors are episodic (cohorts, grants, pitch nights). (nonprofit orgs)
7. **The warmth:** faith-*undertone* community at accessible prices — C12/Convene own faith-*gated* at four figures and $1M–$5M+ revenue. (communities + masterminds)
8. **The funding story:** self-funded via the 10% tithe — the whole category lives (and dies: 1863, Techstars Rising Stars, Echoing Green's deficit) on philanthropy. (nonprofit orgs)
9. **The look:** midnight+gold "quiet luxury" — unowned in this space; Hampton's near-identical palette proves it converts. Carry the architect identity **visually** (gold hairline/construction-line motifs) — the "build/blueprint" *word*-space is crowded. (teardowns)
10. **The banner:** "underestimated founders, Baltimore-rooted" — vacant since Techstars Equitech died (2024) and 1863 closed; UpSurge has dropped "equitech." (DMV)

## The money architecture the market supports

- **Mastermind:** price the "investment invoice" in the **$2,500–$7,500 per 90-day cohort** corridor (anchors: altMBA $4,950/31 days; EO Accelerator $1,750–2,550/yr; Hampton ~$8.5k/yr ceiling). Below ~$1.5k reads like a Skool group; above ~$8k collides with $1M+-revenue buyers. **Keep the application → committee → acceptance-letter flow** — publishing eligibility loudly while revealing price post-acceptance is exactly what Hampton/Vistage/C12 do.
- **Summit:** free; **no paid All-Access Pass in v1**. Community-gated replays convert ~30% of registrants into members vs. 3–5% buying a $47–$197 pass — ~10× the funnel throughput, and it's the exact Robbins/Graziosi AI-Advantage-Summit play (free summit → free Skool → paid tier pitched at the end). Keep the scarcity copy ("replays land in Founders After Hours 24h after each session").
- **Founders After Hours:** free chapter nights (sponsor-underwritten, CreativeMornings-style) + free Skool tier as the funnel + **paid $29–$49/mo accountability tier** (office hours, pods, tally league) — Skool median is $29–$59/mo. Offer a quiet "can't afford it? come anyway" hardship grace — on-brand.
- **Donations/sponsors:** lead with a **goal-as-stat** (the "1M by 2030" pattern) + "Founding Partners" section. Targets: **Capital One** ($265B Community Benefits Plan 2025–29, HQ McLean), **Truist Foundation** (funds nonprofits that strengthen small businesses — Ventriq's exact shape), JPMC, Verizon/LISC, Knight/Kauffman/Surdna/Pivotal; **Techstars Foundation can grant to Ventriq**.
- **Capital pillar shortcut:** become a **Kiva Hub** (0% loans via local host orgs) + AOF referral partnership — real capital substance at launch without building a fund.

## Risks & rules (cross-cutting)

1. **Eligibility wording is a legal artifact post-Fearless-Fund.** The surviving vocabulary: "under-resourced," "underestimated," "underserved," "priority given to" (not "limited to"). Recipe: **identity in story/imagery/outcomes; economics/geography/stage in eligibility.** Justin must have counsel review final language. (nonprofit orgs §wording)
2. **Employer overlap needs a story before launch.** MIC's BRITE serves the same audience free in the same county; Justin's UpSurge navigator role could read as self-dealing. Mitigations documented: employer blessing, Baltimore-city/growth-stage framing, a recusal rule, ally-not-competitor site copy. (DMV §7)
3. **The summit failure mode is vagueness.** One concrete outcome per day, a named audience, and market the 1-hour/day × 2-weeks format as the anti-fatigue feature. Plan week-2 re-engagement. (summit)
4. **Communities die from organizer burnout + "what do I do here?"** Countermeasures: organizer *teams*, certification, one repeatable ritual, a visible metric. FAH's tally is itself the retention mechanic — count outcomes that touch money, not attendance. (communities)
5. **Website discipline:** don't let the donate ask hijack the hero (BGV's failure); embed the donation form on-page; honest launch stats = program facts (dates, seats, hours), not padded numbers; **server-rendered HTML** (1MC/AfroTech serve bots empty shells); one accent color; differentiate any pitch competition from BGV's crowd-voted format. (teardowns + nonprofit orgs)
