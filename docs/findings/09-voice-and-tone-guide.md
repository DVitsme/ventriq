# 09 — Voice & Tone Guide: Writing Ventriq in Justin's Voice

A practical copywriting tool, built from ~35k words of his transcribed speech (2019–2026), his own site copy across four brand eras, his Instagram captions, and his event descriptions. Quote sources per doc 08's legend; ⚠️ video/podcast quotes are auto-captions — verify audio before printing verbatim.

---

## The voice in one sentence

**A warm host with a teacher's structure and a pastor's cadence** — he greets you like family, organizes everything into threes, sells transformation rather than transactions, and closes with a low-pressure invitation.

## His three registers (and where each belongs on the Ventriq site)

| Register | What it sounds like | Him, verbatim | Use it on |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. Practitioner-Teacher** (default, ~70%) | Plain, direct, receipts and percentages, numbered frameworks, defines jargon mid-sentence | "I've closed roughly about 80-90% of my discovery calls following this framework." / "Non-diluted funding is when you have money with no strings attached." | Program pages, Summit, Mastermind, FAQ, how-it-works |
| **2. Preacher-Poet** (peaks, ~25%) | One-line-per-thought cadence, metaphor systems (soil/roots, forge/refinement, gateway), legacy language | "Trust first. Transactions follow." / "You don't skip the roots to get to the fruit." / "Everything visible is sustained by something invisible." | Hero lines, mission, About, Donate |
| **3. Institutional** (sparingly, ~5%) | Applications, committees, acceptance letters, cohorts, graduation criteria | "…the committee is going to review your application… you will receive an acceptance letter… and your investment invoice." | Mastermind mechanics, grants & pitch-competition pages |

**Retired register — do not import:** the 2017–2019 luxury-exclusivity voice ("debonair," "opulent," "If you are to par…"). That belongs to Shaw's Covenant's past, not a nonprofit.

## Signature devices (his fingerprints — use these to sound like him)

1. **Rhyming/alliterative triads** — his most branded move. *"Collaboration, acceleration, and escalation." / "Polished, presentable, and producing results." / "Capital, programming, and council." / "Train the Founder. Forge the Future."* → Use for pillar names and headlines. He thinks in threes.
2. **Contrast couplets ("not just X, but Y")** — *"Not just education, but implementation." / "Build the founder, not just the business." / "Not vanity, but transformation." / "Not just hitting your goals, but exceeding your goals."* → Subheads and value props.
3. **Numbered frameworks** — 1️⃣2️⃣3️⃣ on social, "number one… number two…" in speech, I./II./III. pain lists in web copy since 2019. → Any "how it works" section.
4. **Pain-list openers** (problem → agitate → reframe) — *"The stress starts before you even hit your desk or open your laptop…"* He always names your frustration before offering the answer.
5. **Gateway/access metaphors** — *"Your image doesn't define who you are but it does act as the gateway and the access to opportunities"* → transposed for capital: *"so capital stops being the reason you stall and starts being the reason you scale."*
6. **Journey / walk-beside metaphors** — *"Here's how we can walk beside you." / "Every journey starts with a single step."*
7. **Forge & refinement metallurgy** — *"Forge you into a new founder." / "Iron sharpen iron." /* the diamond monologue: *"it has to go through that refining process of being cut… not by just anything, but by another diamond."* → Perfect for Mastermind & community copy.
8. **Humility prefixes** — *"I have the privilege of being…" / "I've been fortunate to…" / "It's an honor…"* He almost never claims an achievement without one. → Bio and About.
9. **Question CTAs** — *"How do you want to move as a founder?" / "Ready to build differently?"* → Section closers.
10. **Low-pressure invitations** — *"No pressure — just an honest conversation." / "Can't wait to have you."* Never scarcity, never countdown-timer pressure.

## Vocabulary

**His love-list (use freely):** founder · purpose · journey · community · capital · council · clarity · confidence · impact · transformation · implementation · systems · serious · real · honest · legacy · equip · forge · access · opportunity · intentional · sustainable · stewardship · season · elevate · scale

**Handle with care:** *covenant* (equity belongs to the suit brand), *hustle/grind* (he's explicitly anti-burnout), *underrepresented* (his mission — but confirm how explicit he wants it on page one), direct scripture (see faith calibration).

**Avoid:** beast-mode/crush-it hype · get-rich-quick language · "guru/ninja" · exclusivity-for-its-own-sake · unexplained jargon (he defines every term he uses — copy should too) · pressure tactics.

## Faith calibration (important)

The rule he models everywhere: **explicit in interviews, implied in copy — sermonize structure, not scripture.** His IG says "the life you're praying for," not John 3:16. His deepest faith line in program copy is a riff, not a citation: *"Write the plan and make it plain"* (Habakkuk 2:2, uncredited). For Ventriq: values-forward words (calling, stewardship, covering, purpose) are safe; anything overt needs Justin's explicit sign-off.

## Formatting habits (his written tells)

- **Em-dashes for pivots** and a **short punch sentence after a long setup**: "His priority was comfort & aesthetic. **We delivered.**"
- **Direct second person**, present tense: "You're going to…" / "You have to…"
- Occasional **"P.S."** lines with warmth or humor.
- **Emoji as punctuation on social only** (1️⃣2️⃣3️⃣ 🚨 📈 🤝) — **never on the website**.
- He writes fast and ships typos ("there language," "jet steps") — **keep the voice, fix the grammar; that's the service.**

## Dos & don'ts for Ventriq copy

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| Open sections with the reader's pain or a question | Open with Ventriq's own greatness |
| Name pillars and programs in triads | Invent framework names he'd have to relearn |
| Use his receipts (decade in, 100+ events, $1M+ generated) once he approves attribution | Import MIC institutional stats (1,500+ founders) without his sign-off |
| Write CTAs as warm invitations | Use scarcity timers, "only 3 spots left!" pressure |
| Keep sentences speakable — he reads copy aloud naturally | Stack clauses he'd never say on camera |
| Let one human beat live in each section (a "P.S.", a plain aside) | Sand him into corporate blandness |
| Imply faith through values (stewardship, calling, covering) | Quote scripture or preach without his explicit OK |
| Say "underrepresented founders" plainly if he confirms | Hedge the mission into vagueness ("all communities everywhere") |

## Sample copy in his voice (starting drafts for Derrick — not final)

**Hero option A — the triad move** (adapts his own bio line + adds the capital pillar):
> **Train the founder. Fund the vision. Forge the future.**
> Ventriq equips small business owners, startups, and nonprofits — with the capital, programming, and council it takes to build something that lasts.
> [Register for the Summit] [Join the Community]

**Hero option B — his own 2019 line as the hook** (mission-forward, best if he wants the access thesis up front):
> **The resources are out there. They're just camouflaged.**
> Ventriq exists to change that — connecting underrepresented founders to capital, programming, and council, so access stops being the reason you stall and starts being the reason you scale.
> [Register for the Summit] [Join the Community]

**Hero option C — contrast couplet** (quietest, most institutional):
> **Not another program. A covering for founders.**
> Capital. Programming. Council. Ventriq is a nonprofit built to back the builders — the founders doing real work in communities the old models overlook.

**About opening (~90 words, preacher-poet → practitioner):**
> Ventriq started with a conviction our founder has carried since Baltimore raised him: talent is everywhere — access isn't. The resources founders need already exist. They're just camouflaged, waiting on someone to point the way.
>
> So we built the pointer. Ventriq is a nonprofit that equips small business owners, tech startups, and nonprofits with three things: **capital** to build with, **programming** that prioritizes implementation over education, and **council** from people who've actually lived it. Not hype. Not theory. A covering for founders — so you don't have to build alone.

**Summit blurb (~60 words, practitioner):**
> **The Ventriq Summit** — eight sessions, two weeks, one speaker a day. Every session is 70% substance, 30% your questions — and 100% pointed at implementation, not just education. Come for the speakers. Stay for the community: every session ends with a door into Founders After Hours, where the real work continues.

**Founders After Hours blurb (~60 words, forge/community):**
> Founders don't fail from a lack of ideas — they fail building alone. **Founders After Hours** is where that ends: a digital community plus monthly in-person meetups where the first hour is heads-down execution (real calls, real emails, tallied at the end) and the rest is connection. Iron sharpens iron. Come do the work — together.

**Donate blurb (~50 words, stewardship):**
> Every dollar given to Ventriq is pointed right back into founders — funding grants, pitch competitions, and programming for entrepreneurs the old models overlook. If something here brought you value, help us cover the next founder. No pressure — just an invitation to build something bigger than any one of us.

**Why these work:** A uses his triad + "Train the Founder. Forge the Future." DNA; B elevates his most autobiographical access line; C uses his contrast-couplet device and the covering motif (his covenant instinct, secularized). The About walks his Baltimore→access arc without naming scripture; the Summit blurb reuses "not just education, but implementation"; FAH uses his gamified-implementation design and "iron sharpens iron"; Donate echoes "pointed right back out into the community" + his no-pressure CTA pattern.
