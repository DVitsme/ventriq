## The essence

Justin Y. Shaw (b. ~1994–95, Baltimore; ~31) is a 6'5" introvert-turned-institution-builder. As a young Black man in Baltimore who "didn't see value in my voice," he discovered that **image + faith** could be "the gateway to opportunity," turned that revelation into a bespoke suit company at ~22, and has spent a decade climbing altitudes: tailoring individual men → programming entrepreneurial ecosystems → now founding a nonprofit to fund and forge founders. **Every venture is the same product at a different scale: transformation, covering, and access** — first sewn into a suit's lining, now delivered as capital, programming, and council. Ventriq is the endgame he's been quietly tithing toward: *"The vision God called me to is he wants me to shift global economies."* [FCF]

## The story in five movements

### 1) The unseen tall kid (1990s–2015)
Front-lawn toy/book sales at ~8 (hung merchandise from trees); shoveled snow, washed cars; sold candy in high school at "close to $100/day" but wouldn't hire help — *"I didn't trust anyone enough."* Network marketing at 14 via his brother. College dropout. The core wound, in his official bio: *"Growing up in Baltimore, he struggled with his identity surrounded by the promotion of the inferior nature of men of color. In his young adulthood, Justin realized his personal value lied in his image and faith."* Watching his brother: *"Wearing suits gave me a sense of power. It helped build my confidence."* [Voyage, AFRO, SC]

### 2) The awakening (2015–2017)
**Corner Bakery, Pikesville, 2015** — "in a lost state of mind; no longer aware of my purpose." Gilly asks: *"What do you like?"* → *"Well… I like suits… I guess?"* → *"Then just do it."* → *"Figure it out."* Two years pass in a job he hates. Then the **desk prayer**: *"I have nothing to lose and all to gain. If I fail, the worst that could happen is that I'm in $10,000 debt by the time I'm twenty-five. I'm ready to take the risk."* Minutes later, a call granting him $1,000 — *"the timing was too convenient to call it luck. I knew He heard my prayer."* He gives himself **90 days**. **Sept 1, 2017, 7:00 PM:** Shaw's Covenant launches online; booked out two weeks by midnight; **$10K+ by the first week of October**; quits the job. *"I used the money to fund my purpose."* [Voyage]

### 3) The covenant years (2017–2025)
Lyft/Uber **5 AM–noon**, building the business until late evening, "rinse & repeat." Tries to hand-make a tie, quits after 3 hours — *"that wasn't my lane"* — prays, then sources artisan tailors (U.S./England/Italy/China): *"I believe God literally led me to finding the right tailors."* Every suit gets **a faith symbol sewn into its lining** — *"In a world filled with adversity you must always be covered."* COVID: shut ~5 months, surviving on credit cards, clients threatening fraud claims — *"we chose to respond with joy & integrity"*; reopens June 2020, "sales went out the roof," lands the downtown **"Clubhouse"** showroom under market price. Prices climb **$600 (2017) → $979 (2022) → $1,495 (2024–26)**. Suits **Baltimore Mayor Brandon Scott twice (2023)**. **Goldman Sachs 10,000 Small Businesses** alum → Alumni Ambassador; speaks on a stage with George Bush and Chris Paul — *"people were sitting in the audience taking notes on things that I was saying"* and *"I just heard a whisper… this is just the beginning."* Co-authors ***Emerge*** (anthology, ~2019). Community give-back every era: Boys II Men Conference, "Kids of the Covenant." **Oct 2025: pauses the suit brand** — "🚨 Not Accepting New Clients/Orders At This Time." [Voyage, AFRO, SC, FCF, IG]

### 4) The multiplier (2024–2026)
**July 2024:** after 5 months of study, passes his final exam for **multiple finance-industry licenses** — "EVERY investment vehicle available in the U.S. and abroad… to help clients protect & grow their wealth" (a chapter in no bio — but it directly underwrites Ventriq's capital/VC ambitions). Joins **HCEDA — Events Manager → Director of Programming & Partnership at the Maryland Innovation Center**; also becomes a **resource navigator at Upsurge Baltimore** (free startup coaching, Greater Baltimore). **Oct 2025:** registers iamjs.io; **IAMJS** launches — *"Train the Founder. Forge the Future."* Mastermind cohort 1 runs **Jan 5–Mar 30, 2026**. His own receipts, self-authored: *"I've been an entrepreneur for over a decade, built a six figure company, hosted 100+ events for entrepreneurs, and generated over $1M through all of my ventures."* **July 7, 2026** (the day before the kickoff call): launches **The Perennial Edge** podcast with a collaborator (@t_dot_savage) under "The Cultivation Effect" — Mirror • Root • Rise framework, "The Foundry" community. [LI, MIC, IG]

### 5) The covenant at scale (2026 →)
**Ventriq.** On the Feb 2026 podcast he says **10% of all revenue** goes into "a foundation that I'm going to be building out… literally pointed right back out into the community," and that one 2026 goal *"I can't technically say publicly, so I won't right now."* That was Ventriq before it had a name. ## The engine — six motivations (ranked, with his own evidence)

1. **Calling & obedience.** Faith is his operating system, not a marketing layer. *"All that stuff was just a whole lot of obedience… out of obedience I fasted."* / *"If he tells me to do it, I'm gonna do it."* / *"The vision God called me to is he wants me to shift global economies."* Every milestone in his telling is answered prayer. [PoS, FCF]
2. **Dignity & representation.** The Baltimore thesis: kids "damaged by the way the world portrays" them need proof. *"Some of these kids at risk, they just need to see men in suits that come from where they come from and see how they made it."* Image is *"the gateway and the access to opportunities"* — never the definition of worth. Ventriq is this thesis applied to founders. [DDK, PoS]
3. **Transformation over transaction.** *"It's not a transaction for me, it's a transformation."* [AFRO] From suits (*"we're really not here just doing suits"*) to IAMJS (*"we measure what matters — not vanity, but transformation"*). He sells becoming, not products.
4. **Family as the unit of mission.** *"My side of our family's mission and vision is the marketplace… my wife is building a family unit home… we're prioritizing family… and we're not compromising on the quality of life."* This is the root of IAMJS's "founders who don't have to choose." His Facebook bio states the triad plainly: *"Building dreams while keeping faith, family, and good style."* [FCF, FB]
5. **Access & navigation.** The sentence that became his career, said in 2019 about Park Heights youth: *"The resources are here, it's just camouflaged, because they don't have anybody to direct them."* He now literally navigates resources for free at Upsurge — and once told founders: *"If you keep applying for grants, just become a nonprofit"* — advice he then took himself. [DDK, FCF]
6. **Scale with stewardship.** *"I'm not in the business of six figures. I'm in the business of millions. If I'm called to shift global economies, I'm not doing that [at] $100,000."* — held together with the 10% tithe and service-first economics: *"You got to serve the people first before you be able to take something for yourself."* [FCF]

## How he operates — working psychology (for Derrick)

- **Visionary sprinter.** Nonprofit decision → LLC filing → kickoff call inside one week; podcast launched the day before. Vision "evolved into something even kind of greater" between calls. **Design for modularity; expect scope drift; your 10–14-day "fall out of love" runway is exactly the right prescription for him.**
- **Ships fast and imperfect.** Typos live on IG ("there language," "jet steps"); Squarespace placeholder pages sat public for months; two site versions in 8 months. **Your polish is the value-add — but keep one human beat; don't sand him into corporate blandness.**
- **Premium instinct + access mission — resolved by two-tier structures.** Starter vs. "The Covenant" tiers; $1,495 suits alongside free Upsurge coaching; "investment invoices" alongside grants. **Ventriq (nonprofit) + IAMJS/advisory (for-profit) is simply his signature move at entity scale.**
- **Institutional borrowing.** Applications, committee review, acceptance letters, cohorts, certificates, graduation criteria ("hit 80% of your goals… we're not here to make you feel good"). He imports legitimacy structures from Goldman/MIC into his own brands — Ventriq copy should carry that same institutional confidence.
- **Gamification instinct.** Candy-sales tallies → Founders After Hours prospect-call tallies → Mastermind performance dashboard → the simulator dream. Score-keeping is how he makes implementation fun.
- **Teacher compulsion.** Everything becomes a numbered framework; he defines jargon mid-sentence; Socratic by default: *"Nine times out of 10… I ask you questions so you can pull out the answer."*
- **Community is the product — and it's quality-gated.** *"The product is only as good as the people that are inside of it… I don't care if you make $3 million, if you stuck up, I don't want you here. I don't need your money."*
- **Relationship "contact sport."** Follows 4,260 accounts; recruits collaborators (podcast co-host, photographers); clients become testimonials across ventures (Calvin R.: suit client → IAMJS success story).
- **Disciplined about attribution.** Keeps MIC ideas and Ventriq ideas separated; held the Ventriq name off-record for months. He can keep a secret — and expects brand boundaries to be respected.
- **Hands-on AI user.** Brainstorms in Claude (the palette artifact on the call); comfortable reviewing generated drafts.

## What Ventriq means to him

It is the **tithe made structural** (the 10% foundation), the **secret goal made public**, his own **Y Combinator for "the other side"** (underrepresented founders the MIC model doesn't reach), and the **vehicle for the global calling** ("shift global economies… locally, then regionally, then nationally, then internationally"). The pastoral instinct that put a faith symbol inside suit linings — *unseen covering* — becomes a nonprofit that covers founders: capital, programming, council. This is not a side project to him; it's the point of everything else.
