# Ventriq Palette

Three tiers, not six equal colors. (Source: Justin's Claude logo chat, July 2026.)

## Foundation — the base, ~90% of everything
| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Midnight | `#101B2D` | Dominant color; dark backgrounds, wraps the brand |
| Cream | `#F1ECDF` | Light backgrounds |
| Ink | `#0B0F16` | True near-black: body text, one-color logos |

Everyday looks: **midnight background + cream/white content**, or **cream background + ink text**.

## Signature — the one color that says "Ventriq"
| Name | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Gold | `#C9A24C` | The highlight in the mark, the tail on the q, the one warm note against the navy. "If someone remembers one color, it's this." |

## Accent — a spark, ~5–10%, used rarely (PICK ONE — not ratified yet)
| Name | Hex | Read |
|---|---|---|
| Burnt orange | `#C15A2C` | Warm, culturally rooted (Harlem Capital / Essence lane) — provisionally committed |
| Electric teal | `#22B0A0` | Forward / tech — documented as the one-line swap |

Use for: links, buttons, callouts — energy only. "A brand that uses two accents reads as undecided."

## Rules
- The **logo never depends on color**: black on light, white on dark is the master form; color versions only when you control the background.
- Test for anything brand-related: does it survive being **one color**, being **tiny**, and being **on any background**?
- Hexes are a strong starting direction, not gospel — a print designer may nudge gold warmer / navy deeper. The **structure** is what's locked: navy+cream foundation, gold signature, one committed accent.
