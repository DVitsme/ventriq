Ventriq — Brand Usage Sheet

Working copy reconstructed July 8, 2026 from Justin's Claude logo-design chat. Swap in Justin's original usage sheet + SVGs when retrieved.

Ventriq — pronounced "Ven-TREEK" (not "Ven-trick"). Concept: venture + corridor — two lines converging to a vanishing point: a V, a corridor, a threshold. Parent brand above Founders After Hours — slightly more elevated in feel.

1 · Logo hierarchy

  • Wordmark = primary logo. "Ventriq" in one flat color; the corridor tail lives on the q. Most trademarkable asset.
  • Monogram = detachable partner for anywhere the full name won't fit or read: favicon, avatar, app icon, embroidery. The notch cut to the vanishing point is the ownable part — protect and exaggerate the negative space; the moment it reads as "just a V," it stops being ours.
  • Lockup (mark + name) for formal/one-pager use.
wordmark-color — ink + gold tail, light backgrounds
wordmark-white — reverse, dark backgrounds
monogram-duo — ink + gold, light backgrounds
monogram-gold — dark backgrounds / accents
lockup-black — formal use, light backgrounds
lockup-white — formal use, dark backgrounds

2 · The durability rule

Black-and-white first, color second. The master logo is a single flat color — black on light, white on dark — so it survives being faxed, embroidered, laser-etched, or dropped on a photo. Color versions are the dressed-up form, used only when we control the background. The logo never depends on color to be legible.

3 · Clear space & minimum size

  • Clear space: keep at least the height of the monogram's notch (≈ cap-height of the wordmark's "V") free on all sides.
  • Minimum sizes: wordmark ≥ 110px wide on screen; monogram ≥ 16px (it must survive a browser tab).

Reconstructed values — Justin's original sheet specifies these; confirm against it when retrieved.

4 · Do / Don't

Do
  • Use the wordmark first; monogram when space is tight.
  • Keep the gold tail only in the color wordmark on light backgrounds.
  • Use midnight or cream fields behind the logo.
  • Scale proportionally; it's a true vector — favicon to a building.
Don't
  • Don't recolor the logo with the accent or arbitrary colors.
  • Don't soften, round, or shrink the monogram's notch.
  • Don't set the logo over busy photos without a flat scrim.
  • Don't use two accent colors — one, committed.

6 · Typography

Space Grotesk SemiBold for display/wordmark (geometric grotesk — tight, not rounded, not condensed; OFL licensed). The shipped SVGs are outlined, no font install needed. Long-term: commission a bespoke redraw of the letterforms (especially the q) so the wordmark is fully ownable. The monogram is original geometry — ownable as-is.
